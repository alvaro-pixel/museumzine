About

We want to make museum education grow bit by bit

We are here to help you to find more information about museums. You can find resources for reading, watching or listening. Articles have been selected from specific resources and are meant to provide a guide for those learning about museums.

Greetings, my name is Alvaro, I’m a student at the University of London’s CS online program

I made this project for the web development course, I work for a company called museum planning llc so I’m passionate about the museum world and the events that have been taking place since the pandemic. Most of us regard museums at spaces where creativity, openess and learning takes place and it is no surprise the events taking place around the world have deeply affected museum visitorship. This project is both a chance to showcase the learned skills in the web development module and way to to help raise more awarness about museums and their role in our lives.

How to help museums?

Learn from the content, try to reflect on it and if you have questions feel free to contact me:

alvaro@mail.com

How to help local museums

Make at least planned monthly visits to your local museum, visitorship is essential to keeping them alive as well as tighting communities together.

Turning to social media

Make sure you follow their social media accounts, digital presence of museums has expanded in the past year but with your help they can keep digital events and expand online.

Start volunteering

Try volunteering at your local museum, if you are an expert you could help by giving online or live lectures to help museums struggling with their agendas.

How to use this site

Read

Learning about museums is a hard subject but this site has selected material a list of recommened books to get a good grasp of museums.

Listen

For some life is quite busy, but there is a section where you can listen to lectures while taking care of some other activities.

Watch

For most the easiest way to digest information is to watch videos, there is are selected sources of audio/visual material ready for those who want to learn.
