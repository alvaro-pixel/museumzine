National Gallery of Art · Kamala Sankaram and Mark Rothko’s “Untitled”
"Sound Thoughts on Art"

This episode focues on Mark Rothko's art, an artist borned in the backwaters of the late Russian empire. The composer Kamala Sankaram analyses one of Rothko's pieces called, untitled. She guides us through her explorations of grief and the musical score she was creating at the time. Summary by Alvaro Alvarez

Find full transcripts and more information about this episode at https://www.nga.gov/music-programs/podcasts/kamala-sankaram-mark-rothko.html

Published 7 days ago

The Artsy Podcast · No. 2: Art History vs. the Art Market

This episode takes a look at the relation between art history and art market. Art is constantly determined not only by the individual genius of the artist but also by how the market influences the price of works of art. Taste is an separate abstract condition but follows a series of conventions born from patrons and artists as well as art merchants and of course the general public. - Summary by: Alvaro Alvarez

Piece accompanying the episode: https://www.artsy.net/article/artsy-editorial-the-artsy-podcast-no-2-art-history-vs-the-art-market

Solomon R Guggenheim Museum

Published 7 days ago

Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum · Luis Camnitzer, Art History Lesson No. 6, 2000

In this interview Luis Camnitzer, artist from Uruguay who is concerned with art, art as education and consumerism within the museum walls. He presents his workm, that revolves around precarius platforms and found objects that makes us relfect on our history. He tries to explain how our history is constructed as an hegemonic structure and provides a way to reflect on how to create new narratives and different 'art stories'. Summary by Alvaro Alvarez
